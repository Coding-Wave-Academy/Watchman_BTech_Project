The Challenge

Why WatchMan?

Cyber threats are evolving faster than traditional rule-based firewalls can keep up. Universities, SMEs, and research labs often lack the budget for enterprise-grade, intelligent NIDS solutions. Furthermore, audit logs can be tampered with by attackers to cover their tracks.

WatchMan democratizes cybersecurity by providing an open-source, ML-powered detection engine that anchors forensic evidence directly to the Polygon blockchain, ensuring tamper-proof security auditing.

How WatchMan Works

  1. Packet Capture: Sniffs network traffic in real-time.
  2. Feature Extraction: Converts packets into statistical flows.
  3. ML Detection: Random Forest model detects anomalies (DoS, Port Scans, etc.).
  4. Alert Generation: High-confidence threats trigger alerts.
  5. Blockchain Anchoring: Alert batches are hashed via Merkle Trees and stored on Polygon.
  6. IPS Blocking: Malicious IPs are blocked via firewall rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

What operating systems are supported? +
WatchMan is built natively for Linux. We officially support Ubuntu 22.04 LTS and above. The IPS module relies on `iptables` and requires `CAP_NET_ADMIN` capabilities.
Do I have to pay gas fees for the blockchain features? +
By default, WatchMan runs in "Demo Mode" utilizing a local Ganache instance for testing without real gas fees. For production, it connects to Polygon, where transaction fees apply, but we use batching (Merkle Trees) to anchor hundreds of alerts in a single cheap transaction.
Can I train my own ML models? +
Yes. WatchMan includes `train.py` and `cross_validate.py` scripts. You can supply your own PCAP or CSV datasets to train custom Random Forest or Isolation Forest models.
